Drama Phase

Purpose: The purpose of the DRAMA phase is to dramatically re-record the approved translation with better audio quality and more expressive voices. If desired, use multiple voices for the different characters and add sound effects in the background while re-recording the story. Anybody who speaks the language well and has a good voice can be involved in this phase. 

Activities:

  1. Noise control. Get in a place that is quiet and where you will not be interrupted by people or distracting noises. If necessary, use a blanket under the phone and over the heads of those recording to reduce echo or distracting noises.

  2. Listen. For each page, tap the play button at the bottom of the picture. Listen to the approved translation as it plays.

  3. Practice dramatized narration. Listen to, memorize, and practice saying as much of the translation as you can. Tap to pause or resume playback as necessary. 

  4. Record dramatically.  At the bottom of the screen, tap the mic button and re-record the translation with a clear voice and dramatic expression. (Others can add sound effects at the same time.) Tap pause when you are finished.

  5. Practice smaller chunks. Again tap the play button at the bottom of the picture to resume listening and memorizing the next part of the draft.

  6. Resume recording dramatically. To resume dramatic re-recording, tap the mic+ button at the bottom of the screen. 

  7. Finish recording. To end the dramatic re-recording, tap the square stop button at the bottom right of the screen.

  8. Replay recording at any time. Tap the triangle play button at the bottom of the screen to listen to your dramatic re-recording. If you are satisfied with the quality, swipe to the next slide. If you are not satisfied with the quality of the recording, repeat the listening and dramatic re-recording process.

  9. Replay and check the audio for each page.  Before moving to the CREATE phase, start at the Title page and check that you are satisfied with the dramatic audio volume and quality by replaying all the pages one after the other.

  10. Include story transcription or text narration.  For every page, there is the option under the picture to type the audio narration into text.  Play the approved draft and pause to type the words.  Play, pause, and type until each page has been transcribed.  It is suggested that only languages that have a stable orthography or alphabet should do this option. If you want to produce a video that displays the story text as captions on the lower portion of the video, you will need to select the appropriate option in the Create phase. 

  11. Add a song to the end of your story (optional).

  1. Discuss the content and meaning of the story. If possible, write down the main points into a poem. Then compose a song that will help you and others remember the message for this story. Record your song.

  2. Snap a photo of the singers or insert a picture to view while listening to the song.

Notes:

  • To stop a recording, you must tap the square stop button.

  • When a recording is paused, it will resume recording when the mic+ button is tapped.

  • To start a new recording, the mic button must not have a + next to it. Tap the square stop button to refresh the recording button.

  • To resume a recording the mic+ button must be displayed.

  • Sound effects (e.g. crowd noises, chickens crowing) can only be added simultaneously with recording the dramatized narration. The background music which comes with the template usually has some sound effects. 

  • If you find that your newly created video has dubbing mistakes or it does not have good audio quality, you can always return to this phase and re-dramatize any or all of the pages. 

  • If a page is not re-recorded, when a video is created, the approved translation draft is what will play for that page.

Audio Editing with mp3TrueEdit

Story Producer app works with an audio editing app called mp3TrueEdit. Download mp3TrueEdit from the Google Play Store, and install it on your phone. Restart Story Producer. Now, anytime you record audio you will see a pencil icon on the bottom toolbar. Click on it to edit your audio. When you are done editing your audio, choose ‘File | Save Over and Exit’.
