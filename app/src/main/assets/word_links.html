WordLinks

Note: SIL does not currently supply a WordLinks database, but if you have one from another source, it should work in Story Producer.

Who: Translators and Accuracy checkers may find this tool helpful.

Purpose: The purpose of the WordLinks tool is to provide convenient access to difficult words and names across all the stories and to record those local language translations in audio form. This feature can help translators translate important Biblical terms consistently and pronounce people and place names consistently in/across all the stories. It can also help to provide literal text back-translations for all the difficult words and names (and transliterations of names) as another means to help an accuracy checker in their work.

Installation: 

To install a WordLinks database, copy the wordlinks().csv file into the wordlinks folder in the SP Templates folder. Then restart the app.

Activities:

  1. From the sidebar menu, WordLinks list:  

  1. Type in the search bar to find a specific term or name, or scroll through the alphabetized words to find what you are looking for.

  2. Tap a word/name to open the pop-up window for that term.

  1. Tap a hyperlinked term from the text section of a page in the RECORD or ACCURACY phase.

  1. A blue hyperlink indicates that there is no audio rendering for that term. Tapping a blue hyperlink will navigate the user to the research screen for the term.

  2. A clear hyperlink (underlined normal text) indicates that an audio rendering (at least one) has been made for that term. Tapping a clear hyperlink will navigate the user to the audio and text documentation screen for the term. Slide up to see the research screen.

  1. Read and research the meaning of a term. Discuss how to render the term in the context of the story in the local language. Consider other related but different terms.

  2. Document a local language term or word.  

  1. Tap the mic button at the bottom of the screen to record an audio translation of the term in the local language. Tap the square button to stop the recording. 

  2. Tap inside the companion text box to type a word-for-word literal back-translation in the source language (LWC) of the audio rendering for the key term. Or, if a name was recorded, type a (phonetic) transliteration of the word.

  1. Recall how a word was rendered. Tap a clear hyperlink to open the WordLinks pop-up window documentation screen. Tap the triangle play button to listen to how a name or term was rendered in another story. Exit the pop-up window to resume translation work. 

  2. Exit the WordLinks pop-up window by tapping the X in the upper right corner of the screen. Resume your translation and checking activities. Or sometimes, tapping the back arrow on your phone will put you back where you just came from.

Notes:

  • If there is an existing local language Scripture translation or Bible translation team, the same key terms and names should be used so there is consistency in how terms and names are rendered in the stories.

  • Two types of words are included in the WordLinks database:

    • Biblical key terms. Some of these terms may have multiple senses depending on their context. Translators may need to record multiple audio renderings for one key term and discern which rendering to use in which context. Multiple audio renderings might also need to be recorded to document different forms of a term (e.g. verb form and noun form).

    • Names for people and places. The primary purpose for recording an audio rendering for names is to provide consistency in pronunciation across all the stories.

  • There are several sections in the WordLinks pop-up window research screen to aid the story translator in their decision for how to render a term:

    • The hyperlinked word from the story followed by its various grammatical forms. These will be at the top of the pop-up window in the red section. 

    • Notes -- brief definitions or background information.

    • Related (but different) terms, hyperlinked -- to provide some material for comparison and contrast, and further research.

    • Example renderings from other languages, back-translated -- to provide practical ideas for how story translators might want to translate the term in their local language.

  • The recording bar at the bottom of the screen will expand into an extended screen below the research screen once a recording has been made for a key term. Once audio renderings have been recorded for a term, they can be reviewed in this extended documentation screen -- the audio rendering in the local language can be listened to and the associated back-translated text can be read. 

  • The WordLinks local language database should be backed up on a regular basis. The WordLinks folder is located in the root of the SP Templates working folder which may be stored on the phone or on an SD card. For backup, copy the WordLinks directory to another drive or card.  

The author of any WordLinks (Key Term) Database is responsible for its content. Look inside the file to identify its author.
