Share Phase

Purpose: The purpose of the SHARE phase is to view and distribute the videos to others in your language community, whether they are nearby or far away, whether by offline or online means.

Activities: 

  1. View a created video. Tap the triangle play button on the line with a video file name to view that video. You will need to select a video player or photo viewer on your phone.

  2. Constructive review. View a video all the way through before you share it to make sure that it looks and sounds the way it is supposed to. 

  3. Revise faulty videos. If a video has mistakes in it, delete both the mp4 and 3gp for that video by tapping on the trash bin icon with the X. Make revisions in the DRAMA and CREATE phases, and create a new video.

  4. Share it from the Story Producer app. Tap the 3-point open triangle share button to distribute your video. Your phone will bring up several offline and online options for safe sharing:

  1. Bluetooth. Bluetooth is the easiest way to share videos offline one at a time. It may take several minutes to transfer a single video via Bluetooth depending on the video size.

  2. WhatsApp or email attachment. If you have data or Wi-Fi and the ability to connect to the internet, you can share by attaching your video to a WhatsApp message (to a single address or a WhatsApp group) or to an email message (copy multiple recipients).

  3. Upload or post. If you have strong internet connectivity, you can upload your videos to YouTube or Facebook, Google Drive, Dropbox, or your language website. Then share the links with friends. Note that all the created videos are located in a Video folder/directory located in the root of the SP Templates working folder. Normally this is in a hidden folder within Story Producer, and the newer versions of Android will not allow you to see the contents of that folder. To access these files for uploading, you need to do the following:

    1. Open the main menu and choose ‘Select Folder’.  Tap NEXT. 

    2. Create a new folder. Note the name and location. Tap ‘Use This Folder’.  

    3. Allow Story Producer to access files in your new folder. 

    4. Story Producer will give you the option to move your current projects to this new folder. Tap ‘Yes’.  

  1. Copy the videos from your phone to a flash drive or SD card.

      3. Then use the same device to move the videos to another phone.   

  2. Backup all videos regularly. It is a good idea to backup your videos by sharing them with yourself and your community. You can also backup your videos to a flash drive, an SD card, a computer, YouTube, or some other place online (e.g. Google Drive; Scripture Earth).

  3. Use the shared videos in various types of evangelistic, discipleship, and Scripture engagement activities. For example:

  1. Sunday school classes

  2. Men or women Bible study groups

  3. Family devotions

  4. Preparation for sermons

  5. Private viewing and learning

  6. Public showings projected onto a larger screen

  1. Group video activities. Play the video several times for a group and then do the following activities:

  1. Dramatize and retell the story, acting it out.

  2. Learn the song.

  3. Memorize a key verse from the story.

  4. Discuss these questions:

    1. What do you learn about God from this story?

    2. What do you learn about people from this story?

    3. What good and bad examples do you observe from this story?

    4. What life lessons do you learn from this story?

    5. In what way do you need to obey the Lord because you have learned from this story?

    6. What other verses or teaching in the Bible support what you have learned from this story?
