Registration

General:

  1. The Registration screen is generally a one-off activity that is done during the first part of a training workshop or when a local team first begins to use the Story Producer (SP) app.

  2. Tap each information “drawer” to expand it and type in the information. Scroll down to access each field. Tap on a line to place the cursor and bring up the keyboard ready to type information. A few fields are drop down menus which you need to tap on and choose an item. Tap the “drawer” again to close it.

  3. If you SKIP REGISTRATION, you can go directly to the Story Templates list. The next time you open the Story Producer app, the Registration screen will appear. If you SUBMIT the Registration, the Registration screen will be hidden and not appear each time you open the app.

  4. If the Registration information needs to be edited and (re)submitted, it can be accessed by tapping on the top left 3-bar menu icon in any screen which will open a sidebar menu. Tap on Update Registration.

  5. Filling all the fields and submitting the Registration is not a requirement to use the app, but it is helpful to submit the Registration information to assist with future analytics and troubleshooting.

  6. It is suggested that the local SP team leader input the information requested with the help of the SP Trainer. Contact information will need to be provided for the primary translators, the Accuracy checker and the Trainer. The Trainer should be prepared with language information readily available.

  7. The SP Trainer is ultimately responsible for submitting the Registration info via email to SPapp_info@sil.org. When submitting via email, all other emails listed in the Registration fields will automatically be inserted into the email addressee section.

    • If a local SP team phone does not have internet connectivity, the Registration info (a small text doc) can be submitted via bluetooth to the Trainer’s phone. Then when the Trainer gets online, they can resubmit or forward the Registration info on to SPapp_info@sil.org.


Language Information:

  • The Ethnologue Code is a 3-letter code which has been assigned to each language. Tap on the WHAT IS THIS? link to access the online Ethnologue database to search for a specific code. If you are working in a language or dialect that has not yet been assigned a code, please type the 3-letter code of the most closely related language followed by a dash (no spaces) followed immediately by 3 primary letters of the dialect name. For example, PEX-MAJ. This code, if inserted and submitted, will appear in the partially automated title of all videos produced so that it is clear with which language the video is produced.

  • Orthography Status has to do with the newness and stability of the language writing system or alphabet. Tap on the drop down menu and then tap on the most appropriate option.

Translator Information:

  • If you wish to enter information for more than one translator, simply put a comma or a slash and list the multiple people with their corresponding information on the same line.

Accuracy Checker Information:

  • This drawer or section is where information is registered for the person who checks the story for Biblical accuracy. The Accuracy Checker should not be the same person as the Translator. The Accuracy Checker may or may not be the same person as the Trainer.

    • The Accuracy Checker for Bible stories should be someone with the appropriate skills for checking Bible Stories.

  • The Trainer should make sure there is an Accuracy Checker who can assist with approving each story.

  • An Accuracy Checker should be nearby

    • They will be doing in-person accuracy checks on each story, personally holding the SP phone, and controlling the check and any revisions required before approving the story for publication and distribution. Usually a local Accuracy Checker speaks the local language.

Trainer Information:

  • You may or may not have a Trainer involved in your project.

  • If you have a certified SP Trainer involved in your project, his info would go here.

    • People get qualified to be SP Trainers by taking a course or self educating (by working through the unlocked demo story available from the main menu, reading all the Help docs in each phase, perhaps watching some training videos) and passing the “Qualify as a SP app Trainer” Quiz. Contact SPapp_info@sil.org for training options and for access to the SP Trainer Quiz.

    • SP Trainers are responsible for:

      • setting up and training local language teams in use of the Story Producer app

      • connecting local SP teams with an Accuracy Checker

      • training and mentoring other SP Trainers

      • sharing the SP approval password only with Accuracy Checkers

      • submitting Registration information (via email) for teams that they train

  • The Trainer’s location should be the place where the Trainer normally resides.

Additional Contacts:

  • This is where other email addresses can be typed if there is an organization or interested party who wants to receive the Registration information.

  • If an organization or interested party would like to receive produced videos, a whatsapp number or an email address could be entered here to provide a way for the local SP team leader to share videos with them.
