Story Templates

General:

  • Tap on any story template title to open it and begin (or continue) your translation work.

  • You can work on multiple templates, but only one at a time. You don’t have to completely finish a story before starting work on another story.  (Although it is recommended that you try to complete your work in a phase before exiting to work on something else.)

  • If you exit one template to work on another one, your work data will be saved automatically.

  • Any template that you open and close, will open in the same phase (or place) where you stopped before.

  • No matter where you are in the app, you can always tap on the 3-lines menu icon (located top left in all screens) to give you the option to return to the list of Story Templates. (Tap on Story Templates.)

Progress tabs:

At the top of the story list, there are three tabs: ALL STORIES, IN PROGRESS and COMPLETED. The tab which you are currently viewing will be bolded and indicated with a blue line. The default tab is ALL STORIES. This tab lists all the stories which have been scanned into the SP app. To change which tab you would like to view, simply tap on the words labeling the tab.

When you have started to translate a story, as soon as you make the first audio recording in the Translate + Revise phase, this action will cause that story to show up in the IN PROGRESS tab. Also, a blue “highlighting” marker will appear to the right of the story title in the ALL STORIES tab list. So, if you are working on several stories at one time, rather than scrolling slowly through many story templates to find the stories that are in progress, you can quickly scroll and find the blue marker(s), or you can tap on the IN PROGRESS tab to find a short list of the stories that you have started but not yet finished.

Once you produce a video for a story, the SP app will consider that you have completed that story and it will no longer show up in the IN PROGRESS tab; it will show up in the COMPLETED tab. Also, the titles for completed stories will be dimmed in the ALL STORIES Story Template list and the blue marker will become grey. As an app user, you still have complete access to edit stories, produce different versions of a story video and share videos from the Share phase even after it is “completed”.

Filter feature:

If you have all the available story templates installed in your app, it can be a long list to scroll through. The stories can be sorted or filtered by tapping on the white “buttons” at the top of the story list. When a tick mark is showing on a white button, it means those stories are see-able or shown in the list. If there is no tick mark on a button, it means those stories are hidden. Tap the OT button to show or hide Old Testament stories. Tap the NT button to show or hide New Testament stories. Tap the Other button to show or hide other types of (non Bible) stories -- stories used for app training, evangelism and health information etc.
