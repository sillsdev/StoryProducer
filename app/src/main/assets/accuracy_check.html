Accuracy Phase

Purpose: The purpose of the ACCURACY phase is to check and approve that the story translation draft is accurate and meaningful. A person with good Bible knowledge, ideally a local consultant, will hold the phone and facilitate the ACCURACY check. We will call this person the accuracy checker. 

The accuracy checker may request to have one or more local language speakers, who were not involved in the LEARN and RECORD phases, to assist with back translations and answering comprehension and inference questions. One of the original story translators should also be available to make any necessary revisions.

Activities:

  1. Review history logs for each page. The accuracy checker can tap the “sandwich” (three-lines) button on the bottom of each page to see a history log of translation activities which took place in the process of translating that page. Sort this log by tapping the heading of each color-coded column. If the translation and community check are not complete, or if the translation is rough, the accuracy checker should ask the primary translator to complete those activities. 

  2. Listen to the entire translation draft.  Begin with the title page and tap the play button in the middle of the screen. Then swipe to the next page, tap play, and listen. If the accuracy checker does not know the language, the speakers who were not involved in the LEARN and RECORD phases can give an oral back-translated summary of the story.

  3. Comprehend and compare. For each page, the accuracy checker will play and pause as needed to get an oral back-translation phrase by phrase. The accuracy checker can ask any number of comprehension or inference questions until s/he is convinced that the translation for that page is clear and accurate. There should be no misleading information when compared to the source text displayed below the picture and/or when compared to the Bible reference from which the source text comes.  

  4. Review key terms. Discuss and revise key terms as necessary.
    If you have installed a WordLinks database, during the accuracy check, the checker can tap on a hyperlinked (underlined) term to open the WordLinks tool to review the audio renderings and text back-translations/transliterations of the terms in the local language. 

  5. Revise as necessary. If any revisions are needed, the accuracy checker will discuss the issue with the translator(s). The translator can then go back to that page in the RECORD phase and revise that page. Then swipe or tap to return to the ACCURACY phase. 

  6. Tick each page as they are approved. The accuracy checker can tap the grey tick mark button at the bottom of the screen to turn it green when s/he feels a page has been appropriately drafted.

Approve the story. When all the pages have been ticked green, a dialogue box will ask if the story has been properly checked for accuracy. When the ”YES” is tapped, the app will unlock the DRAMA phase and other production phases.
